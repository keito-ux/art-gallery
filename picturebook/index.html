About the Story

One quiet night, a boy meets a glowing star fairy in his room. Entranced, he follows her into a world of wonder. Guided by a gentle reindeer, he journeys through enchanted forests and silent skies, planting a seed that grows into a blooming garden — a symbol of hope and light.

Chasing the star is the journey of following your true calling — the quiet pull of your destiny.
Growing a garden teaches us this: you don’t catch the butterfly by chasing it, but when you gently tend your space, it finds you.

Behind the Scenes

This project began with two people — Keito from Japan and Yulianna from Ukraine — who first met in Kyiv and later started co-creating online. What started as a simple exchange of art and stories became a heartfelt mission — to create a picture book that crosses borders, supporting Ukraine and opening a space for shared expression.

Keito Nakanishi

A senior student at Sophia University in Tokyo, majoring in science and engineering. In August 2024, I backpacked through Eastern Europe and was deeply moved by the spirit of the Ukrainian people. That winter, I traveled to Ukraine, visiting Kyiv and Lviv. Although self-taught in oil painting, I create works that reflect quiet beauty and cross-cultural connection — shaped by my travels, encounters, and a deep desire to express warmth through art.

Our Vision

This project began with a quiet hope — to support Ukraine, to share the voices and expressions of artists, and to create a gentle space where creativity brings people together.

✧ Two Pillars ✧

  • 1. Creating a picture book that blends storytelling, art, and hope.
  • 2. Inviting and sharing the voices and expressions of Ukrainian and international artists who resonate with this mission.

🇵🇭 Riann Quintos

Hi, I’m Riann — a mixed media artist from the Philippines. I work mostly with simple, everyday materials like cheap ballpoint pens, highlighters, alcohol markers, gouache, and acrylics. My art often revolves around moments that quietly stay with me: mundane scenes, fleeting visuals, and emotions that feel both oddly specific and strangely familiar. Much of what I create is shaped by the media I consume, the experiences I live through, and the thoughts that tend to linger in my head a little longer. Art, for me, is a way of holding on — to feeling, memory, and presence.

🇺🇦 Elina Ellis

Elina Ellis is a Ukrainian-born illustrator, author, and educator currently based in Cambridge, UK. She began her artistic journey after a career in business and earned an MA in Children’s Book Illustration from Anglia Ruskin University.
Elina is the author and illustrator of acclaimed picture books such as The Truth About Old People and Chickadee: Criminal Mastermind. Her work has received international recognition, including the Macmillan Prize (2017) and Golden Pinwheel Grand Award (2018).
Combining digital and traditional techniques, her warm and humorous style brings stories to life and resonates with readers around the world.

🇬🇧 Lisa House

Lisa is based in the Northeast UK and creates atmospheric, textured landscape & still-life paintings. Inspired by nature, travel and local surroundings, her work is a delicate balance of instinct, emotion, and skill. Her soulful approach allows her to create pieces that are deeply immersive, symbolic and have a dreamlike quality on both a conscious and subconscious level. Alongside her landscapes, Lisa combines inanimate objects with birds and animals, creating still-life collections filled with character and charm. Her exploratory mixed media technique and use of fabrics are distinctive elements in her work, making it recognisable to art collectors worldwide. Her most recent work welcomes new symbolic elements experienced and inspired by dreams, meditation practice and sound baths.

🇺🇸 erin kate archer

Artist Bio: Erin Kate Archer
Erin Kate Archer (she/her) is a New York-based artist whose work stands out as a beacon of resilience and solace. Erin's artwork is a soothing amalgamation of the ethereal and the tangible, narrating tales of endurance and the feminine spirit through her mixed-media paintings.
Her portfolio is a testament to her commitment to tranquility amidst struggles with chronic illness and pain. It features immersive fairytale landscapes, celestial pieces, and intricate depictions of femininity, womanhood, and community.
Erin's artistry extends beyond personal expression. Erin's accomplishments include being published in 'She Votes' (2020) and 'Forget Me Not Press - A Cold Winter's Night' (2022), and exhibiting in several prominent NYC art shows in 2023, such as Studio Artego's summer show and The Art of Access benefit show.
Artist Statement: Erin Kate Archer
In my art, I seek to create a haven for those in search of comfort, inspiration, and magic. My work is a celebration of the transformative power of creativity and the essence of femininity, an invitation to explore a world where there is strength in softness, where the challenges of life are met with resilience and grace.
Through my mixed-media paintings, I use color and texture to make a space where viewers can find solace from their struggles. My work is a reflection of my journey, a narrative woven with threads of my own experiences with chronic illness and pain, and a testament to the therapeutic power of art.

🇫🇷 Maria-Victoria Checa

In my impressionist landscapes, I aim to bring forth elation and serenity. I rarely use a paint brush and work mostly with painting knives and other tools like squeegees. Armed with my fancy squeegees and various painting knives, I like to create colorful imaginary forests and landscapes in oil, acrylic or watercolor.
A recurring comment on my social media has been the uplifting and cheerful nature of some of my paintings. Nothing makes me happier!
Up until January 2022, I was teaching Math at American University and Montgomery college, while dedicating the rest of my time to painting. I have shifted my priorities and become a full-time artist. I believe this has allowed me to make tremendous progress in my painting skills and to discover unexplored inner realms of the soul. Every morning, I wake up, go to work and look forward to the new adventures awaiting me in my studio!

🇺🇸 Sarah Mason

Hi, I’m Sketchbook Sarah—a painter and visual storyteller exploring memory, girlhood, and the strange beauty of everyday life. Raised in Colorado and trained at RISD, my work blends surrealism, symbolism, and personal narrative. I paint trippy, emotional worlds inspired by nature, feminism, and my own history, often weaving in themes like family, femininity, and healing. Whether I’m working on canvas or in my sketchbook, I aim to make art that feels both intimate and otherworldly—like a dream you half-remember but can’t shake.
For me, art is a way of healing and expressing the parts of myself I don’t see anywhere else. I encourage everyone to put themselves out there in anyway that resonates.

🇺🇦 Anastasiia Kopach

My name is Anastasiia Kopach, a sculptor who graduated from the Lviv National Academy of Arts. My works explore internal questions and often use abstraction to reflect the realities of life
This flower, with its gently cheerful and soft essence, is my attempt to find joy in the creative process. While traditionally drawing flowers can sometimes feel repetitive, I sought a different path. By embracing oil pastels, smudging, and scratching, I found a dynamic and interactive way to bring this piece to life. For me, the unexpected discoveries in such a process are far more captivating than a perfectly idealized result.

🇫🇷 Taevane

I’m a self-taught collage artist, deeply shaped by an early life immersed in art. My father was a still life oil painter and teacher, giving lessons in a studio at the back of our garden. From a young age, I watched him share his love of light, color, and detail.
At thirteen, I discovered collage as my own medium—and I never stopped. My process is intuitive and emotional, guided by a neurodivergent perspective that sees connections between images, symbols, and stories.
Through collage, I aim to evoke feeling, build bridges between cultures and sensibilities, and offer quiet fragments of light and hope in a complex world. I just made this collage. I know it’s not only flowers, but I thought of Ukraine’s spiritual and Christian heritage. The sparkling flowing water symbolizes renewal, grace, hope, and the continuous flow of life even through hardship.
